SBI brings together researchers in synthetic biology, dynamical systems, microbial ecology, genome engineering and quantitative measurement. The institute integrates diverse experimental platforms with modeling and computation to understand and engineer living systems across scales from single cells to complex microbial communities.

## Engineered Gene Circuits and Cellular Computation

**Aim:** Build, analyze and control genetic circuits that carry out programmed behaviors inside living cells.

Focus areas:

- Design principles for regulatory networks  
- Synthetic oscillators, toggles and signal processing modules  
- Noise, stochasticity and feedback in gene expression  
- Approaches to maintain robust circuit performance in fluctuating environments

## Multicellular and Microbial Community Engineering

**Aim:** Control interactions between cells and species to construct stable, responsive and functional communities.

Focus areas:

- Synthetic communication channels and quorum systems  
- Division of labor strategies for task allocation  
- Spatially structured microbial consortia  
- Tools for tuning community stability, competition and cooperation

## Cellular Plasticity, Differentiation and Reprogramming

**Aim:** Understand and engineer the transitions between cell states, integrating genomics and dynamical modeling.

Focus areas:

- Mapping cell state landscapes using transcriptomics and imaging  
- Circuit based control of differentiation and identity  
- RNA velocity, trajectory inference and dynamical state modeling  
- Cellular memory and adaptive responses

## Quantitative Dynamics and Theory Driven Design

**Aim:** Develop mathematical and computational frameworks for guiding the design of synthetic systems.

Focus areas:

- Modeling of gene network dynamics and signaling pathways  
- Stochastic simulation and coarse graining of biochemical systems  
- Control theory approaches for biological regulation  
- Hybrid physics based and data driven models for prediction and design

## Microbiome Engineering and Host Microbe Interactions

**Aim:** Engineer microbiomes and host associated microbial systems with applications in health and environmental resilience.

Focus areas:

- Synthetic approaches to modify community composition  
- Mechanisms linking microbes to host physiology and metabolism  
- Tools for analyzing high dimensional microbial and metabolomic data  
- Therapeutic strategies using engineered strains or consortia

## High Throughput Technologies and Measurement Platforms

**Aim:** Develop tools that enable precise, scalable and automated interrogation of biological systems.

Focus areas:

- Microfluidics for single cell and community level control  
- Automated culturing platforms and real time imaging  
- Computational pipelines for genomics, proteomics and metagenomics  
- Experimental methods for measuring cellular dynamics and interactions

## Translation, Applications and Emerging Directions

**Aim:** Connect foundational synthetic biology to real world challenges.

Focus areas:

- Biosensing for diagnostics and environmental monitoring  
- Engineered microbial therapeutics  
- Biomanufacturing and programmable metabolism  
- Design of systems that interface with natural microbiomes or host tissues
